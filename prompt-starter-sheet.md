# Dental Growth Suite — Prompt Starter Sheet

## 10 Ready-to-Use Prompts

Copy and paste these prompts directly into any Dental Growth Suite assistant. Customize the bracketed sections for your practice.

---

### 1. Monthly Social Media Content

**Assistant:** Zeta Dental

**Prompt:**
```
Create 30 social media posts for a [family/cosmetic/emergency] dental clinic in [city]. Include a mix of:
- Oral health tips and educational content
- Promotional posts about [cleanings/whitening/Invisalign/implants]
- Engagement posts that encourage comments
- Awareness posts about [oral cancer/dental anxiety/children's dental health]
- Patient testimonial-style posts

Target audience: [adults/families/seniors/young professionals]
Tone: [professional/friendly/approachable]
```

---

### 2. SEO Blog Post

**Assistant:** Lex Dental

**Prompt:**
```
Write an SEO blog post for a dental clinic in [city] targeting the keyword "[service] in [city]." 

Service: [Invisalign/dental implants/teeth whitening/emergency dentistry]
Target word count: 800-1000 words
Include: Introduction, what the service is, benefits, process overview, cost considerations, and a call-to-action to book a consultation.
```

---

### 3. Google Business Profile Posts

**Assistant:** Terra Dental

**Prompt:**
```
Create 10 Google Business Profile posts promoting [service/offer] for a dental clinic in [city].

Service: [professional teeth whitening/dental implants/Invisalign consultation/new patient special]
Include: Short engaging copy, clear call-to-action, and relevant hashtags where appropriate.
Mix of: Educational, promotional, and urgency-driven posts.
```

### 10. Patient Education Post

**Assistant:** Lex Dental or Zeta Dental

**Prompt for Educational Blog Post:**
```
Write a patient education article about [topic] that's easy to understand but medically accurate.

Topic: [gum disease/early signs of oral cancer/how cavities form/the importance of regular cleanings/dental anxiety and how to manage it]
Target audience: General public, ages 25-65
Tone: Educational, reassuring, not alarmist
Include: What it is, why it matters, prevention tips, when to see a dentist, and a soft CTA.
```

**Prompt for Social Media:**
```
Create an educational social media post about [topic] that teaches patients something valuable.

Topic: [proper brushing technique/why flossing matters/foods that stain teeth/how often to replace your toothbrush]
Include: Hook that grabs attention, 3-4 key points, and a question to encourage engagement.
```

## Tips for Better Results

### Be Specific

✓ "Create posts for a family dental clinic in Denver targeting parents"
✗ "Create dental posts"

### Include Context

✓ "Tone should be professional but friendly, targeting busy working professionals"
✗ "Make it sound good"

### Define Your Audience

✓ "Target audience: Parents of young children, ages 30-45"
✗ "For everyone"

### Mention Services

✓ "Focus on Invisalign, teeth whitening, and family dentistry"
✗ "Our services"

### Set the Tone

✓ "Professional but approachable, anxiety-reducing for nervous patients"
✗ "Dental tone"

## Customization Checklist

Before using generated content, customize:

- [ ] Practice name
- [ ] Location/city
- [ ] Specific services offered
- [ ] Contact information
- [ ] Current promotions or offers
- [ ] Photos of your actual practice and team
- [ ] Any specific compliance requirements for your region
- [ ] Your practice's unique voice and personality

## Advanced Tips

### Chain Assistants Together

1. Use **Solis** to plan your monthly theme
2. Use **Zeta** to create social posts for that theme
3. Use **Lex** to write a supporting blog post
4. Use **Terra** to create GBP posts promoting the blog

### Repurpose Content

- Turn a blog post into 5 social media posts
- Expand a popular social post into a full article
- Use FAQ content for GBP posts

### Batch Create

Set aside one hour per month to:
1. Plan next month's theme with Solis
2. Generate all social content with Zeta
3. Create GBP posts with Terra
4. Write one blog post with Lex

**Remember:** These prompts give you strong starting points. Always review, customize, and add your practice's unique personality before publishing.
